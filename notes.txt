1. SOA Design Principles (L R A A D C S S)
L – Loose Coupling (Design services with minimal dependencies so changes in one service do not impact others.)
R – Reusability (Build services that can be reused across multiple applications to avoid duplication.)
A – Autonomy (Each service should operate independently and manage its own logic and data.)
A – Abstraction (Hide internal implementation and expose only what is necessary through APIs.)
D – Discoverability (Ensure services are easy to find using documentation or registries.)
C – Composability (Combine services together to create larger business workflows.)
S – Statelessness (Each request should be independent without relying on stored session state.)
S – Scalability (Design services to scale independently based on demand.)

2. AWS CAF (B P G P S O)
B – Business (Align cloud adoption with business goals and ensure it delivers value.)
P – People (Develop the skills, roles, and culture required for cloud adoption.)
G – Governance (Apply policies and controls to manage risks and ensure compliance.)
P – Platform (Provide the cloud infrastructure, applications, and data environment.)
S – Security (Protect systems and data using identity, encryption, and monitoring.)
O – Operations (Run and manage systems effectively through monitoring and incident response.)

3. AWS Well-Architected Pillars (O S R P C S)
O – Operational Excellence (Run systems efficiently using automation, monitoring, and continuous improvement.)
S – Security (Protect data and systems through strong identity and threat detection controls.)
R – Reliability (Ensure systems recover quickly from failures and remain available.)
P – Performance Efficiency (Use resources efficiently and scale to meet demand.)
C – Cost Optimization (Minimize cost by using only the resources you need.)
S – Sustainability (Reduce environmental impact through efficient resource usage.)

4. AWS Cloud Design Principles (S T A M M D C U)
S – Stop guessing capacity (Use metrics and Auto Scaling instead of predicting capacity needs.)
T – Test in production (Validate systems using real-world techniques like canary releases.)
A – Automate everything (Automate infrastructure, deployments, and operations.)
M – Make small, frequent changes (Reduce risk by releasing small and frequent updates.)
M – Monitor everything (Continuously track logs, metrics, and system behavior.)
D – Design for failure (Build systems assuming failures will happen and ensure resilience.)
C – Cost optimization (Continuously manage and reduce cloud costs.)
U – Use serverless when possible (Leverage managed services to reduce operational effort.)

👉 “Run containers without managing servers”
Serverless compute for containers (ECS/EKS). No EC2 management needed. You define CPU/memory and AWS runs and scales it.

👉 “Track and analyze AWS costs”
Shows where your AWS money is going and how discounts are applied.

👉 “Automated data transfer”
Moves data between on‑prem, AWS, or AWS storage services (fast and repeatable).

AWS Application Migration Service
👉 “Lift-and-shift servers”
Replicates and migrates on‑prem servers to EC2.

AWS Systems Manager
👉 “Manage servers centrally”
Control EC2 and on‑prem servers without SSH/RDP. Useful for hybrid environments.
